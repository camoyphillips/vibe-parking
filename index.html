Reverse Parking Instructions:
  1. The setup of your vehicle is the most important!
  2. Signal and stop beside the parking spot, leaving about 1 meter of space (imagine someone standing between your car and the edge of the parking line).
  3. Indicate and shift to reverse and check mirrors and blind spots.
  4. It is better to reverse with a wide turn (easier to pull forward and correct) than narrow here.
  5. Slowly back up checking both mirrors, turning the wheel full lock toward the spot, the rear wheel should be about 10 inches from the line.
  6. Straighten the wheel when the rear wheel is exactly parallel to the line, adjusting as needed.
  7. Center your car in the space and stop when fully inside.
  8. Shift to park and apply the parking brake.
Parallel Parking Instructions:
  1. The setup of your vehicle is the most important!
  2. Signal and stop beside the car (leaving about 1 meter of spaceâ€”imagine someone standing between your car and the other car or cone) in front of the parking space, aligning your rear bumper with theirs.
  3. Shift to reverse and check mirrors and blind spots.
  4. Turn the wheel sharply toward the curb and back up slowly.
  5. When you are aligned with the other vehicle's rear bumper, turn your wheels two times to straighten your wheel.
  6. When your front bumper clears the rear bumper of the car ahead (you can see the rear license plate), stop when your vehicle's rear wheel is 10 inches ("about your shoe size") away from the curb.
  7. Now, pivot the vehicle, full lock to the left until the rear wheel is parallel to the curb.
  8. Then stop and center in the space.
  9. Shift to park and apply the parking brake.
